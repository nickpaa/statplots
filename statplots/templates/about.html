What's this about?

I deal with statistical problems in my everyday life:

  • My work involves statistical questions like "any idea what a negative binomial with mean 0.8 and standard deviation 1.7 looks like?"
  • I've found it surprisingly difficult to plot a distribution if you know its mean and variance but not its parameters
  • Speaking of parameters, I always forget how to parameterize different distributions in different settings:
    • How exactly do you parameterize the negative binomial again?
    • Are we doing the gamma as shape and scale? Or shape and rate?
    • Is the exponential parameter the mean? Or its inverse?
    • Well, are using Python? R? Excel? Something else?
    • Python, eh? Hm, sometimes I get confused converting to Scipy's parameterization...
  • I get into debates with strangers on the train about Bayesian priors, and it would be handy to display distributions on a mobile device.

Thanks to the economic implications of COVID-19, I've had some additional free time and have started learning web development in order to build this site. I'm mostly a Python and analytics guy, so this has been a fun challenge.

This site is a work in progress. More to come:
