What's this about?

I deal with statistical problems in my everyday life:

  • My work involves statistics, and we ask ourselves "any idea what a negative binomial with mean 0.8 and standard deviation 1.7 looks like?"
  • I always forget how to parameterize different distributions in different settings:
    • How exactly do you parameterize the negative binomial again?
    • Are we doing the gamma as shape and scale? Or shape and rate?
    • Does the exponential use lambda? Or its inverse?
    • Well, are you in Python? R? Excel? Something else? The answer will probably be different.
  • I get into debates with strangers on the train about Bayesian priors, and it would be handy to display distributions on a mobile device.

I've also got a quarantine goal of learning web development. I'm a Python guy and have a decent background in analytics, but I'd like to grow my skills.

This site is a work in progress. More to come.
